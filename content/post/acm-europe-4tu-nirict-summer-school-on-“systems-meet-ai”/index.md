I am pleased to share that we are organizing the **ACM Europe & Dutch 4TU.NIRICT Summer School on “Systems Meet AI”**, which will take place in **Delft, the Netherlands, from July 6–10, 2026**. The summer school will be hosted at the **Echo Building, Delft University of Technology**.

The summer school focuses on the growing synergy between **artificial intelligence** and **networked computing systems**. This interaction is becoming increasingly important: on the one hand, modern AI workloads require efficient, reliable, and scalable computing and networking infrastructures; on the other hand, AI and machine learning techniques are increasingly used to improve system design, resource management, and system operation. The school therefore centers around two complementary perspectives: **Systems for AI** and **AI for Systems**.

Through lectures and discussions with experts from across Europe, the school aims to provide students and early-career researchers with a broad view of how AI-powered systems can be designed and deployed in an energy-efficient, reliable, and scalable manner. Application domains include areas such as healthcare, 6G, and industrial automation.

I am happy to be part of the organizing committee together with colleagues from TU Delft, TU/e, UTwente, Wageningen University & Research, the University of Amsterdam, and Leiden University.

This initiative is closely connected to our broader effort to build a Dutch and European research community around the intersection of AI and computing systems. I look forward to welcoming participants in Delft and to the discussions that will emerge from this summer school.

More information is available on the summer school website: https://www.systems-ai.nl/
